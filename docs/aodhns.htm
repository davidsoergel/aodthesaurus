SN Use these descriptors to index documents about a field, discipline, profession, or occupation. For example, index a document on the "History of Psychology" with *+SF psychology (field)* qh ah combined with *+PN history* qh ah. Do not use these descriptors for documents that deal with the subject matter of a field; for example, index a textbook "Introduction to Psychology" with *+FA psychology* qh ah.
      More specifically, use descriptors from this area in the following circumstances: (1) The social, political, or economic structure; the history; the role; or the ethics of the field or profession is discussed (combine with descriptors from *+M social sciences, economics, and law* qh ah); (2) curricula and other aspects of education in the field are discussed (combine with descriptors from *+NF education and training* qh ah); (3) research organization and management, including information services and funding, in the field are discussed (combine with descriptors from *+RB research organization and management* qh ah or from *+ND2 information transfer* qh ah); (4) people in the field, profession, or occupation are discussed (combine with descriptors from *+TT status by occupation* qh ah or a narrower descriptor or with other appropriate descriptors from *+T demographic characteristics* qh ah).

SN These fields overlap widely. They are both concerned with birth defects and abnormal development.       Teratology has been defined as follows: The study of abnormal development. More particularly it is the study of the causes, mechanisms, and manifestation of abnormal development whether genetically, gestationally, or postnatally induced; and whether expressed as lethality, malformation, growth retardation, or functional operation.

SG14.4.  preventive medicine (field)   qh
SN That specialty of medical practice which focuses on the health of individuals and defined populations in order to protect, promote, and maintain health and well-being and prevent disease, disability, and premature death. (American Board of Preventive Medicine). Preventive medicine includes or draws on the following areas:
      -biostatistics
      -epidemiology
      -environmental and occupational health
      -planning, administration, and evaluation of health services
      -the social and behavioral aspects of health and disease
      -the practice of prevention in clinical medicine.
SN Care which has the aim of preventing disease or its consequences. It includes health care programs aimed at warding off illnesses (e.g., immunizations), early detection of disease (e.g., Pap smears), and inhibiting further deterioration of the body (e.g., exercise or prophylactic surgery). Preventive medicine developed following discovery of bacterial diseases and was concerned in its early history with specific medical control measures taken against the agents of infectious diseases. Preventive medicine is also concerned with general preventive measures aimed at improving the healthfulness of the environment. In particular, the promotion of health through altering behavior, especially using health education, is gaining prominence as a component of preventive care.
