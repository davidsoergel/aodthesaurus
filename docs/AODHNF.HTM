FAepsychology   d-out   qh
SN Psychology studies mental processes, such as perception, cognition, memory, and emotions, as well as individual behavior. Psychology is also concerned with the physical basis for these processes; thus, it overlaps with biology. Hence, some descriptors needed to index psychological topics are found in *+E concepts in biomedical areas* qh ah. Conversely, individual mental processes and behaviors are influenced by and interrelate with the social context; thus, psychology overlaps with the social sciences. Hence, some descriptors needed to index psychological topics are found in *+M social sciences, economics, and law* qh ah, particularly including the common subfield of *+LA social psychology* qh ah.

FB10e.  theory of reasoned action   qh
HN Introduced 2000.
SN The theory of reasoned action (TRA or TORA) provides an account of the way in which attitude toward a behavior (which takes into account the evaluation of various characteristics of the behavior and the importance of each characteristic), subjective norm (which takes into account beliefs on what other persons or groups think about behavior and the motivation to comply with these persons or groups), and behavioral norm combine to predict behavior. The theory of planned behavior (TPB) suggests that perceived behavioral control can influence behavior indirectly through intentions, with motivational implications.
