MP18.2.8.12.  .  .  drug decriminalization   qh
SN The repeal of laws or regulations that define the behavior, product, or condition concerning illicit drugs as criminal. Also used with regard to alcohol, such as the crime of public drunkenness. Also sometimes applied to the reduction in the seriousness of the crime or its penalties, as when possession of marijuana is downgraded from a crime warranting arrest and a jail term to an infraction to be punished with a warning or fine. Thus decriminalization is often distinguished from legalization, which involves the complete repeal of any definition as a crime. Decriminalization also is often a state effort to control or influence the market for the affected behavior or product.

MP18.2.8.16e.  .  .  harm reduction policy   qh
SN In an alcohol or other drug context, this term describes policies or programs that focus directly on reducing the harm resulting from alcohol or other drug use. The term is used particularly to refer to policies or programs that aim to reduce the harm without necessarily affecting the underlying drug use; examples include needle-syringe exchanges to counteract needle-sharing among heroin users and self-inflating airbags in automobiles to reduce injury in accidents, especially as a result of drinking and driving. Harm reduction strategies thus cover a wider range than the dichotomy of supply reduction and demand reduction.

MP18.4.  public policy on pharmaceuticals   qh
SN The system of regulations intended to affect the marketing, availability, and therapeutic use of medicines. Often used in the more restricted sense of government control of the pharmaceutical industry and market. Policy on psychoactive drugs is normally an important component, reflecting the fact that a large proportion of all prescriptions that are written are for such drugs. The World Health Organization's list of essential drugs is an effort to assist developing countries with formulating a pharmaceutical policy attuned to allocating scarce funds for pharmaceuticals by health needs rather than by market considerations.
