TZ2.2e.  high-risk youth   qh
SN Youth that experience multiple risk factors at high levels. The Anti-Drug Abuse Act of 1986 (Public Law 99-570), Section 509A [a] defines high-risk youth as those who: are economically disadvantaged; are runaways or homeless; are school dropouts; are pregnant; have mental health problems; have attempted suicide; have parents who are substance abusers; are victims of physical, sexual, or psychological abuse; are involved in violent or delinquent acts.
      Places to find high-risk children or youth for selective prevention: (1) Children living in high-risk families, (2) children of abusers of AOD in drug treatment or self-help groups. (3) Children of mentally and antisocial parents in treatment. (4) children of criminally involved parents. (5) Children living in neighborhoods of high crime and poverty. (6) physically and sexually abused children referred to protective services. (7) immigrant children experiencing high immigration stress and conflict with their parents.
